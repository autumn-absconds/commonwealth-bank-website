Tips and Tricks for Improving Financial Cybersecurity

  • Keep Software Up-to-Date:

    Cybercriminals develop new ways to attack your computer constantly. Ensure your operating system, web browser, and security software are set to update automatically. Out-of-date software can leave your computer vulnerable to malware, which can be used to compromise your accounts.

  • Use Security Software:

    Install reputable security software from well-known companies. This software helps protect your computer from various threats. Make sure it's set to update automatically to defend against the latest threats.

  • Be Wary of Pop-Ups:

    Don't buy security software in response to unexpected pop-up messages or emails, especially those that claim your computer has malware. Scammers use these tactics to trick you into purchasing unnecessary or harmful software.

  • Treat Personal Information Like Cash:

    Your personal information, including Social Security numbers, credit card details, and bank account numbers, is valuable. Only provide this information to trusted sources. Be cautious when asked for personal information, whether in emails, texts, or on websites.

  • Verify Companies:

    Research companies before sharing personal information or making transactions. Use search engines with keywords like "review," "complaint," or "scam" along with the company name. Bad reviews can be a red flag, and if you can't find contact information for the company, consider other options.

  • Use Encrypted Websites:

    When shopping or banking online, use websites that encrypt your data during transmission. Look for "https" at the beginning of the web address to ensure a secure connection. Check for this on every page you visit, not just the sign-in page.

  • Secure Your Passwords:

    Create strong passwords with at least 10 characters, mixing letters, numbers, and special characters. Avoid common words, names, and birthdates. Don't use the same password for multiple accounts, and never share passwords via phone, text, or email. Keep your passwords in a secure place.

  • Enable Multifactor Authentication:

    Whenever possible, use multifactor authentication (MFA) for added security. MFA requires two or more types of verification, such as a password and a code sent via text message, to access your accounts.

  • Back Up Your Files:

    Regularly back up important files to a removable disc or external hard drive. This ensures you can recover your data if your computer is compromised.

  • Be Vigilant:

    Be aware of signs indicating a problem with your account, such as unfamiliar transactions, missing funds, or unauthorized updates. If something seems amiss, contact your financial institution immediately.
